At-Risk Depression Prediction Using Social Media Data

An Application by Ishan Mysore

Introduction

  • In this digital age, the prevalence of social media platforms in adolescents' lives is linked with a rise in depression.
  • A study on American teens aged 12-15 found that those using social media for over three hours daily faced twice the risk of negative mental health outcomes, especially depression.
  • The risk of depression increases by 13% for each hour increase in social media use in adolescents.
  • Media influence and gender norms can worsen the discrepancy between an adolescent's actual experiences and their perceived notions for the future, causing the adolescent to have an inadequate perception of themself.

How This Application Can Help

  • This application will analyze your Twitter (X) or Instagram social media account.
  • Using a machine learning model that's been trained on 350,000+ depressed and nondepressed social media posts, the application will assess the frequency of posts in your account that contain elements of depression.
  • Based on this assessment, the application will predict if you are at risk for depression.
  • If necessary, the application will lead you to some resources or suggest that you seek an actual diagnosis from a mental health professional.
  • Failure to address depression during the adolescent stages may result in more severe consequences toward adulthood, impairing both physical and mental health and limiting adults from achieving their full potential.

This application should never be used as a substitute for professional medical evaluation or treatment. The predictions made by this model are probabilistic and should be used only to highlight the need for a more thorough evaluation by a healthcare provider.
